# StackNest — Design Context

**Product**: A Chrome new-tab replacement / tab & bookmark manager. Stash open
windows into collections, organize into Spaces, tag links, find & remove
duplicates, unified search, glanceable market ticker.

**Target audience**: Power users and knowledge workers with heavy tab/bookmark
load who want to declutter and organize their browser. They open this dozens of
times a day — it must feel calm, fast, and out of the way.

**Use cases**: Stash a window's tabs in one click; browse/organize collections
on a kanban board; tag & cross-reference links; hunt down duplicates; quick
keyboard-first search; check crypto/FX at a glance.

**Brand personality / tone — "Nothing":**
- Monochrome. Colour is reserved almost entirely for the user's own data
  (Space / collection identity dots). The chrome itself is greyscale ink.
- Engineered & precise: hairline borders, dot-matrix texture, tracked-out
  monospace micro-labels, everything on a 4px grid.
- Minimal, uncluttered, zen. Generous whitespace. Flat — no coloured glows.
- Professional and quiet. Nothing shouts.
- Flawless in both light (ink on paper) and dark (chalk on ink). The primary
  action inverts between themes — solid black button in light, solid white in
  dark — the monochrome signature.

**Type**: Hanken Grotesk (UI) + JetBrains Mono (labels, counts, data). Mono is
used *purposefully* as tracked-out engineered micro-labels, never as generic
"techie" body text.

**Spacing scale** (`--s1..--s9`): 4 · 8 · 12 · 16 · 20 · 24 · 32 · 40 · 48.
**Radius scale** (`--r1..--r6`): 6 · 8 · 10 · 12 · 14 · 16.
Nothing chrome (the app frame) uses ink; user data keeps its colour dots.
